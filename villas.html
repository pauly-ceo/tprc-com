Eight private villas. One operating system.

Ananta Villas Bukit is a compact estate of eight detached villas at Desa Kutuh, the elevated southern tip of Nusa Dua. Each villa is held individually by its owner, designed and built to a single architectural standard, and operated end-to-end by The Personal Resort Company.

The project sits behind Kempinski Bali and the upcoming Mandarin Oriental, on the same plateau that already hosts Sofitel and Bali National Golf Club. Thirty minutes from Ngurah Rai International. Walking distance to the cliff path above Pantai Gunung Payung.

Seven of eight villas are placed. One remains. Designed by Studio Identity Designers. Built by THIERRA Construction. Operated by TPRC.

Architecture & design

Stone, glass, water. The simplest possible plan.

Studio Identity Designers — the practice behind some of Bali's most recognised contemporary villas — drew the brief tight: two floors, a courtyard, a pool, and a kitchen that opens straight onto it. Volcanic stone walls, full-height glass to the bedrooms, vertical sky lights above the staircase.

Each villa stands alone on its plot, oriented to keep sightlines off the neighbour and to push the sun across the courtyard from dawn to sunset. There is no shared corridor, no shared lobby, no shared pool.

THIERRA Construction is delivering the build under a fixed-scope contract. Two villas are at structure stage today. Handover is sequenced through Q3 2027.

The site is on Jl. Gunung Payung 8, Desa Kutuh — the southernmost ridge of Nusa Dua. Direct neighbours are Kempinski Bali 5★ and the upcoming Mandarin Oriental, scheduled to open in 2027. Sofitel and Bali National Golf Club are minutes away. Garuda Wisnu Kencana cultural park sits above, Benoa Harbour below.

The plateau is gated, quiet, and zoned for tourism — there is no traffic noise, no through road, no nightlife strip. The cliff path above Pantai Gunung Payung is a five-minute walk from the villa door. Ngurah Rai International is thirty minutes by car, less without traffic.

Design notes

Four design moves that define the villa.

01 · Water

Private pool

Pool runs the full width of the courtyard, edge-flush with the living room threshold. No shared water, no fence between living and swimming.

02 · Glass

Full-height glass bedrooms

Three meters of glazing in every bedroom, opening to the courtyard. Curtain and shutter layers allow full privacy without losing the view.

03 · Stone

Stone-island kitchen

Single block of local volcanic stone, three metres long, doubles as island and dining bar. Opens directly onto the pool deck.

04 · Light

Vertical sky lights

A tall slot of glass cuts down through the staircase from roof to ground floor. The villa is lit from above, not from the front.
