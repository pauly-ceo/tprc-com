Property

Three buildings. Thirty-six homes. One operating system.

Ananta Canggu is a compact residential cluster on a 1,400 m² plot in Padang Linjong — four minutes by scooter from Echo Beach, eight on foot, and thirty-five to forty-five minutes from Ngurah Rai International. Three buildings, thirty-six residences, 1,884 m² saleable, 891 m² built-up.

The brief was deliberately small. Studio Identity Designers drew compact, design-led homes — three unit types, two outdoor moves, three rooftops — that sit inside Canggu's planning envelope without fighting it. Each residence is held individually by its owner; THIERRA Construction delivers the build under a fixed-scope contract.

Once the keys turn in Q2 2028, the buildings come online under TPRC operation. One brand standard, one P&L per residence, one rental program. Owners read a clean monthly statement; we run the resort.

Architecture & design

A small footprint, an organic skin, three rooftops.

Studio Identity Designers held the project tight: a single plot, three slender buildings, and a vegetal facade that handles the heat instead of fighting it. The structures sit low against the palm line, with bougainvillea cascading over the upper edges and a continuous run of window planters carving the elevations into vertical bands.

Inside, each residence is single-occupancy and single-storey, with a private outdoor move on every floor — a garden with hot tub on the ground level, planted alcoves on the second, rooftop access on the third. Three communal rooftops divide the leisure programme by mood: Wellness, Social, and Active. A full-width infinity pool runs along the top of the cluster.

THIERRA Construction is delivering the build under a fixed-scope contract, breaking ground Q3 2026 and sequencing handover for Q2 2028.

Signature elements

Six moves that define the building.

01 · Skin

Organic facade

An undulating screen of vertical timber and planted reveals breaks the elevation into bands of light and shadow. The skin handles the Canggu heat and the Canggu camera.

02 · Ground

Palm gardens

The ground plane keeps the existing palm line and threads new tropical planting through it. Coconut, frangipani, heliconia — three layers of canopy, ground to sky.

03 · Top

Three rooftops

Wellness, Social and Active. Three distinct programmes — sunrise yoga, day-bed pool, gym and stretch zone — separated by building so owners and guests pick their mood.

04 · Colour

Bougainvillea cascade

Mature bougainvillea trails from the upper edges of each building, picking up the heat in spring and the camera year-round. The only saturated colour in the project.

05 · Water

Rooftop infinity pool

A full-width pool along the top of the cluster, edge-flush with the planted parapet, oriented to catch the late-afternoon light off the Echo Beach line.

06 · Frame

Window planters

A run of integrated window planters wraps the second floor, framing each opening with green. Privacy without curtains, view without exposure, screen without a wall.

Keys

The site sits in Padang Linjong, the residential ridge that backs Canggu's surf coast. Echo Beach is four minutes by scooter, eight on foot. Old Man's, Batu Bolong, Pererenan and Berawa — the four breaks that define this stretch — are all inside a fifteen-minute scooter ride. Tanah Lot temple is twenty minutes north along the coast road.

Day-to-day, the neighbourhood reads as the most operationally serious cluster on the island: La Brisa, FINNS Beach Club and Atlas Beach Fest within a single ride, the deepest concentration of speciality coffee and co-working in Indonesia, and a year-round restaurant scene that holds the rate up through low season. Indonesia recorded 6.95 million international arrivals in 2025; Canggu absorbs a disproportionate share of the repeat-visitor cohort.

Ngurah Rai International is thirty-five to forty-five minutes by car, depending on traffic. The site is gated, set back from the main road, and zoned for tourism use.

Frequently asked

Top ten questions.

01 · Leasehold structure and duration

Each residence is held on a leasehold of thirty years with a contractually pre-agreed thirty-year extension, signed and notarised at handover. The leasehold is registered with the Bali land office in the owner's own name — there is no nominee structure and no PT PMA requirement.

Foreign nationals can hold leasehold title directly under Indonesian law. The structure is the same one used by every major five-star branded residence on the island.

02 · Source and realism of the projected ROI

ADR and occupancy are modelled against the Bali Home Immo Q1 2025 Canggu submarket report and the Bukit Vista 2024–2025 operating dataset. Macro inputs cross-check against BPS Statistics Indonesia tourism data, which recorded 6.95 million international arrivals in 2025.

The MID scenario uses 70 percent occupancy and 256 rented nights per year — both conservative against published Canggu averages. The HIGH scenario reflects peak-season-weighted operating data from comparable TPRC-managed units in the submarket.

03 · Profit share and rental program split

TPRC charges a flat twenty percent management fee on gross rental income. Operating expenses — utilities, daily service, marketing distribution and ongoing maintenance — are passed through at cost on a transparent monthly statement.

There is no separate marketing fee, no booking commission stacking, and no minimum-nights clause that holds the unit off the rental pool. The owner receives a single monthly net figure with full underlying detail.

04 · Currency and foreign-exchange risk

The SPA is denominated in US dollars. Construction is contracted by THIERRA in Indonesian rupiah with USD hedging built into the fixed-scope agreement, so the buyer is not exposed to construction-side FX drift between SPA and handover.

Post-handover, gross rental income is collected in a mix of currencies through the distribution channels (USD, IDR, AUD, EUR) and net-settled to the owner in their preferred currency monthly. Owners can also elect to hold a USD balance with TPRC and draw monthly.

05 · Exit strategy and resale liquidity

Secondary-market resale of Canggu leasehold residences is a well-established route in Bali. TPRC maintains an internal buyer waitlist and an outbound partner network across Europe, the Gulf, and Singapore that surfaces qualified resale demand.

Owners can list at any time after handover. TPRC handles the listing, qualified-buyer screening, leasehold transfer paperwork and notarisation against a flat one percent placement fee on the sale price.

06 · Owner usage — fourteen free nights per year

Each owner is entitled to fourteen complimentary nights per calendar year in their own unit, bookable through a private owner portal up to nine months in advance. Nights are stay-only — utilities and daily service are passed through at cost.

Owners can also book additional nights at a flat thirty percent discount on the published nightly rate, subject to availability. There is no minimum-stay restriction on owner usage.

07 · Construction delay protections

The SPA includes a liquidated-damages clause at zero point one percent of the contract price per day of delay beyond the Q2 2028 handover date, capped at five percent. THIERRA's fixed-scope build contract sits behind the same LD schedule.

All milestone payments are held in a Bali notary escrow and released against verified construction certificates from an independent third-party surveyor. No payment moves on the developer's say-so.

08 · Resale before completion

Pre-handover assignment of the SPA is permitted with TPRC's written consent — withheld only against payment status, not the resale price. There is no anti-flipping clause: an early reseller keeps their realised uplift in full.

TPRC will surface the unit to its internal buyer waitlist and partner network on the same terms as a post-handover resale, against a flat one percent placement fee on the assignment price.

09 · Ongoing costs — service charge, taxes, utilities

The service charge covers shared-area maintenance, security, landscaping, the three rooftops, and the rooftop infinity pool. Indicative cost is $1,800 per unit per year, billed in two semi-annual instalments. Utilities are metered per unit and passed through at cost.

Indonesian property tax (PBB) is approximately 0.1 percent of assessed value per year. Income tax on rental yield is handled through TPRC's licensed local accountant — typically a final 10 percent withholding for non-resident owners.

10 · What happens if TPRC stops operating

The leasehold title and the underlying SPA sit with the owner directly, not with TPRC. If TPRC were to cease operating, the residence remains the owner's property and the operating contract can be assigned to any qualified operator on the island.

TPRC's operating contract is renewed annually and terminable with ninety days' notice by either party. The owner is never locked into a long-form operating agreement they cannot exit, and the property is never structurally dependent on TPRC as an entity.
